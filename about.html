The beginning of formal technical education in India can be dated back to the mid-19th century. Major policy initiatives in the pre-independence period included the appointment of the Indian Universities Commission in 1902, issue of the Indian Education Policy Resolution in 1904, and the Governor General’s policy statement of 1913 stressing the importance of technical education, the establishment of IISc in Bangalore, Institute for Sugar, Textile & Leather Technology in Kanpur, N.C.E. in Bengal in 1905, and industrial schools in several provinces.

who we are

Project Showcase is an online integrated platform for displaying project reports of the students. It showcases the projects, also the abilities and skills of the people involved in it. Committing to sharing student work with the public is a great accountability measure because Project Based Learning performances are likely to require demonstration of a broader skill set including not only content knowledge, but also skills like collaboration, creative problem solving, communication and creativity. It enhances peer learning and cross functional research between various universities/colleges as it a great source of knowledge. It encourages student community to take up unique/innovative project works.
